28.5.2023 @ 7:00 - Aging

Been eating porridge for days like a real sick person. Didn't sleep for a night, then got a cold yesterday (god forbid me to go out for some fresh air) and slept for approximately 13.5 hours.

Finding it hard to make peace with the fact that I can get sick, burnt out, hungry, or anything of the sort. It's hard to believe that I can attempt to ruin my health and actually suffer consequences from it. I hate thinking that I'm not young and reckless anymore.

I always wanted to die clean and pretty, I don't want to be slowly marching towards death. I don't want to be the ship of Theseus where each part of the ship is replaced with a dysfunctional version of itself, and then the worse-functioning version of that one, until eventually the ship is rendered completely useless and unrecognizable from its younger self.

I mean, of course, that the ship of Theseus is a silly problem. Whether the ship is continuously aged into a less-functioning version of itself, or enhanced and repaired into a better functioning version, it would remains "the same ship" for as long as people want to believe so. It's because identity is a social construct and if a social contract assigned the identity "ship of Theseus" onto this random ship, then in turns, only a social contract could remove it.

Caramelldansen, a tale of lost media, historical revisionism, and balsamic vinegar.

Youtuber jan Misali made a 45 minutes video called "Who wrote Caramelldansen?" in which he attempted to trace down the originality of the song from being an average pop song from the (now-disbanded) Swedish band "Caramell", to a hit nightcore remix, to a japanese meme on Nico Nico Douga, to now being credited to Swedish-owned "Caramella Girls"

Maybe in a sense, the Caramella Girls are the modern incarnation of Caramell. They've just been Ship-Of-Theseus-ed into a lineup that no longer has any of their original members. Except it's not really like the Ship Of Theseus. It's more like... if you were to disassemble a ship one piece at a time, but instead of replacing those parts as you take them out, the ship is just left without those parts, and eventually, the ship is completely gone. Cookies disclaimer

The Jukebox on /~147/ is powered by Bandcamp and Great DJ, which means you might receive Bandcamp and YouTube cookies (Great DJ is based on YouTube). I'm sure most people are cool with Bandcamp cookies, since they don't collect personal data. Youtube cookies, on the other hand, pose a bigger concern to many people. So if you're about to squeam...

YouTube is malware! It's owned by Google and tracks its users!

I'm aware. I'm not a fan of Google trackers either, but in the case of YouTube, I'd say that the benefit outweighs the risk. I like YouTube's community of creators and its database of free, accessible media, so I'm not gonna stop using YouTube anytime now. What I cannot stand is actually the interface of YouTube[dot com], that's why I use the embed player instead of just linking to YouTube. What I'm doing here is effectively taking the YouTube content away from the YouTube interface, to make a point that although I love the content, I don't necessarily endorse the platform.

For me, the truly most unbearable things about YouTube[dot com] are, among others:A) the ads (especially mid-video),
B) the addictive UI (e.g endless scrolling, recommendation, etc) and
C) the gatekeeping (e.g region block, background playing block on mobile devices, etc). These have been geting worse in the last few years thanks to YouTube's reinforcement. They are no longer "strings attached to a free service", they are red flags from an intrusive, manipulative, attention-grabbing, greed-mongering service owned by Silicon Valley lizards.

Fortunately, there are ways to work around or circumvent most of these problems using the embed player and/or the YouTube API. There are no ads in YouTube embeds (last time I checked), no distracting UI elements (when the player is small enough - here it is max 355px), and in a continuous playlist where one video plays after another, there's no time for recommendation. The background playing problem could be solved using the YouTube API.

Great! So why didn't you just write up your own player using the API? Why Great DJ?

Using the API would mean writing JavaScript, and I intended to write no JavaScript for /~147/. Could I have written a vanilla HTML/CSS player? Why, of course, I gave it a try. You can see the demo here.

I found out that without using JavaScript, the player barely works. I mean, it does work but it'd usually throw a few errors before doing what it's intended to. But then there are problems with videos that block embeds. Plus, I want to be able to use the jukebox as background music player, e.g on my phone, and there's no way to bypass the background playing block without using JavaScript.

So even though I hate having dependencies, I now rely on Great DJ to handle my YouTube embeds. I try to link Bandcamp instead of YouTube whenever possible.
